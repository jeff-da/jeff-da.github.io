I think I thought about it more, and I know more about why I feel the way I do about the culture here and the people here. When I was a kid, I was always bullied for being the short asian kid with the weird skin. I was never really accepted, and the people who did seem to be my friends would always leave when it got inconvient. So I got to college, and I was really excited. It was a new start - college was supposed to be fun, the people were supposed to be nice, the expirence was supposed to be lifechanging. I wanted to have faith in people - on the first day of school, I walk around all eight floors of my dorm and knock on every door. I ask them to join me in my valiently naive quest for friends. There's a big group of us at the start - but after a couple weeks, people start to drift off. And after a few more months, even more people start to drift off. People start making new group chats and not inviting me to them. This happens again when I get in my major - and I join leadership to try and change the culture here. It doesn't work. So I intern in London to see it it's only a local thing. I think I'm just slowly starting to give up. I learned that there are really really good people out there, and there are people that I really really like and that I really get along with. But just that they're rare, and that I need to treasure them, because they're really hard to find.
